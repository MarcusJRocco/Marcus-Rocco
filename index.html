Luxury Retail Generative-AI Recommendation Engine

Leading a five-member Agile team to design and prototype a generative-AI stylist delivering personalized product recommendations for The Webster.

Summary

Designed and prototyped a generative-AI recommendation engine that acts as a digital stylist for The Webster. The system combines a sampled snapshot of live product catalog data with synthetic customer and influencer datasets to generate tailored, in-stock product recommendations with clear rationales aligned to shopper intent and brand identity.

Problem Context

Luxury e-commerce often lacks the high-touch curation found in boutique retail environments. Shoppers expect context-aware discovery based on event, style preferences, brand affinity, and influencer taste, without navigating large product catalogs. This project translates The Webster’s curated identity into an AI-assisted experience that personalizes discovery while preserving brand voice, merchandising standards, and inventory constraints.

Tools & Skills

  • Python (pandas, data validation, descriptive statistics)
  • Large Language Models (GPT) (prompt design, intent alignment, few-shot patterns)
  • Agile Delivery (scope definition, sprints, stakeholder reviews)
  • Data Engineering (data sampling, synthetic data design, pipeline checks)

Data & Constraints

  • Product Data: Sampled snapshot of a live product catalog used to ensure realistic inventory attributes, pricing, and availability.
  • Customer & Influencer Data: Synthetic datasets used to model shopper preferences, style signals, and influencer–brand relationships.
  • Influencer Weighting: Followed influencers boost associated brands and categories within the ranking logic.
  • Event & Intent Signals: Occasion tags such as wedding, vacation, work, and streetwear guide filtering and prioritization.
  • Catalog Stability: Snapshot-based product feeds ensure consistent model behavior throughout development and evaluation.
  • Governance: No personally identifiable information (PII) is used; all outputs remain brand-appropriate and non-speculative.

Approach

  1. Data Preparation & Enrichment:
    • Standardized product attributes including brand, category, color, material, seasonality, and price from the live catalog snapshot.
    • Created synthetic customer profiles and mapped influencer style focus to brands and categories to support ranking adjustments.
  2. Intent Modeling:
    • Derived event and style signals from natural-language user queries. Applied filters for budget, category, color, availability, and relevance to narrow candidate recommendations.
  3. LLM Prompt & Behavior Design:
    • Structured outputs as ranked recommendation tables with concise “Why it works” rationales. Enforced tone, naming, and explanation rules to maintain brand consistency and response reliability.
  4. Prototype Implementation:
    • Deployed a custom GPT configured with structured prompts, guardrails, and data boundaries to simulate customer journeys and support stakeholder demos.
  5. Evaluation & Iteration:
    • Reviewed recommendation quality and response consistency through scenario testing and stakeholder feedback. Iterated on prompt structure, ranking weights, and filtering logic to improve alignment with merchandising expectations.

Expected Results & Impact

  • Functional Prototype Delivered: Completed a working generative-AI stylist capable of producing on-brand, intent-aware product recommendations using a live product catalog snapshot combined with synthetic customer and influencer signals.
  • Transparent Recommendation Logic: Implemented a clear ranking and rationale framework balancing customer intent, brand alignment, influencer weighting, and inventory constraints.
  • Stakeholder Enablement: Supported live demos and walkthroughs that allowed business and product stakeholders to interact with the system and evaluate AI-assisted personalization concepts.
  • Implementation Insights: Clarified data requirements, governance considerations, and system boundaries needed to support future production deployment and scaling.

Content Acquisition Optimization for Streaming Profitability

Modeled film licensing decisions under budget and diversity constraints to identify a profit-maximizing content portfolio for a streaming platform.

Summary

Built a decision-support optimization model in Excel to guide film licensing decisions for a streaming platform. The model used binary integer optimization implemented through Excel Solver to identify an optimal 34-film portfolio that maximized projected profit while satisfying real-world financial, portfolio size, and content diversity constraints.

Problem Context

Streaming platforms face intense competition for subscribers while managing high licensing costs and finite content budgets. Content acquisition decisions must balance profitability with portfolio breadth, as overconcentration in genres or directors can reduce long-term engagement. This project addressed the challenge of selecting a minimum viable set of films under strict budget and revenue constraints, while explicitly modeling trade-offs between profit maximization and content diversity.

Tools & Skills

  • Excel (Solver – Evolutionary Algorithm)
  • Binary Integer Programming (Knapsack Optimization)
  • Predictive Modeling (Cost & Revenue Estimation)
  • Data Cleaning & Scenario Analysis

Data & Constraints

Dataset: Approximately 4,800 films sourced from Kaggle and filtered to the top 100 most popular titles for optimization analysis.

  • Key Variables: title, genre, director, budget, popularity, revenue.
  • Constraints:
    • Licensing Budget: Maximum total licensing cost of $405M.
    • Minimum Revenue: Required portfolio revenue of at least $500M.
    • Target Profit Margin: Minimum profit margin threshold of 19%.
    • Minimum Film Count: Portfolio required to include at least 25 licensed titles.
    • Diversity Constraints: Penalty functions applied for repeated genres and directors to encourage portfolio variety.

Approach

  1. Data Cleaning & Preparation:
    • Cleaned and standardized the source dataset, removing incomplete records and retaining the 100 most popular films to focus the optimization on commercially relevant titles.
    • Ensured consistency across key attributes including budget, popularity, and revenue.
  2. Popularity Normalization & Scoring:
    • Constructed a normalized popularity index on a 0–1 scale.
    • Used this index to generate popularity-based adjustment factors that informed consistent cost and revenue estimation across films.
  3. Predictive Modeling:
    • Estimated licensing costs as a function of production budget and normalized popularity.
    • Modeled potential streaming revenue using normalized popularity and historical revenue signals to create comparable financial proxies.
  4. Optimization Design:
    • Defined binary decision variables representing licensing choices.
    • Incorporated constraints for budget, minimum film count, revenue thresholds, and diversity penalties to reflect real-world acquisition trade-offs.
  5. Solver Execution:
    • Formulated an objective function to maximize total projected profit, defined as estimated revenue minus estimated cost and diversity penalties.
    • Used Excel’s Evolutionary Solver to evaluate thousands of possible portfolios and select the optimal 34-film solution.

Results & Impact

  • Optimal Portfolio Selection: Identified a profit-maximizing portfolio of 34 films spanning 32 genres and 27 directors.
  • Financial Performance: Generated approximately $229M in projected profit with an estimated profit margin of approximately 40%.
  • Diversity Trade-offs: Applied approximately $9.75M in genre and director penalties, illustrating how high-return titles can offset diversity costs.
  • Decision Insight: Demonstrated that quantitative optimization can surface non-obvious trade-offs between profitability and content variety.
  • Strategic Value: Produced a reusable decision-support framework that reduces guesswork in high-stakes content acquisition planning.

Delta Air Lines Star-Schema Data Warehouse & BI

Designed a data warehouse and business intelligence (DW/BI) architecture for a passenger airline environment using dimensional modeling to support operational reporting and decision-making.

Summary

Designed a multidimensional data warehouse and business intelligence solution for a passenger airline division. The architecture incorporated a high-level and detailed bus matrix, conformed dimensions, star schemas, transformation rules, and a multidimensional database (MDDB) layer, enabling role-based Tableau dashboards for analysts, operational teams, and managers.

Problem Context

Airline operations involve high-volume, interdependent processes including flight operations, booking and ticketing, crew management, baggage handling, maintenance, and loyalty programs. When data is siloed across systems, organizations struggle to produce timely, consistent insights. This project addressed the need for an integrated DW/BI foundation to standardize KPIs, improve operational visibility, and support data-driven decision-making across airline functions.

Tools & Skills

  • Tableau (role-based dashboards, KPI design)
  • Draw.io (ERD design)
  • Dimensional Modeling (bus matrix, conformed dimensions, star schema)
  • ETL & Transformation Rule Design
  • Aggregate Tables & MDDB Cube (performance layer for fast analytics)
  • ERD and Data Warehouse Architecture Design

Data & Constraints

Data Source: Flight operations and performance data sourced from the Delta Virtual Airlines Statistics platform.

  • Subject Areas: Flight operations, booking and ticketing, baggage handling, crew management, delay management, loyalty, maintenance, and support.
    • Each subject area modeled as a distinct fact table with a clearly defined grain and associated measures.
  • Core Dimensions: Date & Time, Route, Aircraft, Pilot/Crew, Flight Status, Airport, Fare Class, Booking Channel, and Weather.
  • Grain Example (Flight Operations Fact): For flight operations, one row per flight by route, with measures including delay duration, flight length, distance traveled, and fuel consumption.
  • Design Constraints:
    • Conformed dimensions across subject areas for consistent KPIs.
    • Star schemas aligned to clearly defined grain and surrogate keys.
    • ETL stages with transformation rules for data standardization.
    • Aggregate tables and an MDDB cube to accelerate common analyses.

Approach

  1. Business Process Discovery & Scoping:
    • Identified core airline processes and analytical priorities using a high-level bus matrix, opportunity matrix, and prioritization grid.
  2. Dimensional Modeling:
    • Developed a detailed bus matrix and star schemas with conformed dimensions to harmonize metrics across operational domains.
  3. Logical & Physical Design:
    • Defined fact table grain, surrogate keys, measures, and dimension hierarchies to support consistent aggregation and reporting.
  4. ETL & Transformation Rules:
    • Specified data cleansing, conformance, and derivation rules to standardize disparate source data into a unified warehouse model.
  5. Performance Layer:
    • Designed aggregate tables and an MDDB cube to accelerate common flight-operations and performance analyses.
  6. User & Task Analysis → Dashboards:
    • Built Tableau dashboards tailored to analysts (drill-down analysis), operational teams (situational awareness), and managers (KPI tracking and resource planning).

Results & Impact

  • Integrated Analytics Foundation: Implemented conformed dimensions that ensured consistent KPIs and definitions across airline operational domains.
  • Operational Visibility: Enabled analysis of flight delays, high-traffic routes, airport performance, and operational bottlenecks through standardized dashboards.
  • Decision Support: Delivered role-based Tableau dashboards supporting planning, operations, and executive-level insight.
  • Performance Optimization: Improved query performance and dashboard responsiveness through aggregate tables and multidimensional modeling.
  • Scalable Architecture: Established a reusable DW/BI design capable of extending to additional subject areas such as maintenance and loyalty analytics.

New Medical Device Profitability Simulation

Performed a Monte Carlo–based “what-if” analysis to estimate first-year profit potential and assess financial risk for MedicTronix’s new medical device.

Summary

Conducted a first-year profit simulation for MedicTronix’s new medical device to quantify expected profitability and downside risk under uncertainty in demand and unit costs. The analysis applied a Monte Carlo “what-if” approach with 1,000 simulated iterations to model revenue, costs, and profit outcomes.

Problem Context

MedicTronix required a structured approach to forecast first-year profitability for a new medical device amid uncertainty in direct labor costs, parts costs, and market demand. Traditional point estimates obscure risk and variability, so a probabilistic model was developed in Excel to simulate a wide range of possible financial outcomes and support an informed investment decision.

Tools & Skills

  • Excel (RAND, VLOOKUP, NORM.INV, AVERAGE, STDEV.S, COUNTIF)
  • Monte Carlo Analysis (1,000 iterations of what-if analysis)
  • Scenario Modeling & Risk Analysis (profit distributions & input ranges)
  • Statistical Analysis & Data Visualization (frequency table, profit distribution chart)

Data & Constraints

Dataset: Simulated operational, cost, and demand data based on market research and financial estimates for MedicTronix’s new device.

  • Key Parameters (Expected Values):
    • Selling Price per Unit: $2,495
    • Administrative & Advertising Costs (fixed): $5,000,000
    • Direct Labor Cost per Unit (mean): $1,400
    • Parts Cost per Unit (mean): $350
    • First-Year Demand (mean): 25,000 units
  • Range Inputs (Simulation Ranges):
    • Direct Labor Cost per Unit: $1,200 – $1,600
    • Parts Cost per Unit (uniform distribution): $250 – $500
    • Demand (normally distributed): 0 – 50,000 units

Approach

  1. Model Design & Parameterization:
    • Defined a probabilistic profit model linking revenue, variable costs, and fixed costs into a single profit equation.
    • Incorporated uncertainty through randomized sampling of labor costs, parts costs, and demand.
  2. Simulation Execution:
    • Ran 1,000 Monte Carlo iterations to generate a profit distribution capturing expected value, variability, and extreme outcomes.
  3. Statistical Evaluation:
    • Computed descriptive statistics including expected profit, standard deviation, minimum and maximum profit, and probability of loss.
  4. Visualization & Reporting:
    • Produced a profit frequency distribution to visualize probability density across simulated outcomes and support interpretation of risk and upside potential.

Results & Impact

  • Expected Profit: Estimated first-year profit of approximately $13M.
  • Downside Risk: Observed a 0% probability of loss across simulated scenarios.
  • Outcome Range: Simulated profit outcomes ranged from approximately $3.7M to $25.9M.
  • Most Likely Outcome: Profit clustered around approximately $15M across the most frequent simulation results.
  • Decision Support: Analysis supported a recommendation to proceed with development and prototyping based on strong profitability and resilience under cost inflation scenarios.
