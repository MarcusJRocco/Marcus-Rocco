Prototype Generative-AI Recommendation Engine

Leading a five-member Agile team to prototype a generative-AI stylist that delivers personalized product suggestions for The Webster.

Summary

Designing and prototyping a generative-AI recommendation engine that acts as a digital stylist for The Webster. The system uses synthetic customer, product, brand, and influencer datasets to generate tailored, in-stock recommendations and clear rationales aligned with shopper intent and style.

Problem Context

Luxury e-commerce often lacks the high-touch curation of boutique retail. Shoppers expect context-aware discovery (event, style, brand affinity, influencer taste) without wading through large catalogs. This project aims to translate The Webster’s curated identity into an AI-assisted experience that personalizes discovery while preserving brand voice and merchandising standards.

Tools & Skills

  • Python (pandas, data validation, descriptive statistics)
  • LLMs / GPT (prompt design, intent alignment, few-shot patterns)
  • Streamlit (interactive prototype for real-time scenarios)
  • Agile Delivery (scope, sprints, stakeholder reviews)
  • Data Engineering (synthetic data design, pipeline checks)

Data & Constraints

Dataset: Synthetic datasets for Customer, Product, Brand, and Influencer (no real data used).

  • Influencer Weighting: Followed influencers boost associated brands/categories in ranking.
  • Event/Intent Signals: Occasion tags (e.g., wedding, vacation, work, streetwear) guide filtering.
  • Catalog Stability: Product feed snapshotting to ensure consistent model behavior during development.
  • Governance: No personally identifiable information (PII) is used in any dataset. All outputs and explanations must remain brand-appropriate and non-speculative.

Approach

  1. Data Preparation & Enrichment:
    • Standardize product attributes (brand, category, color, material, season, price) and create synthetic customer profiles.
    • Map influencer style focus to brands/categories for ranking boosts.
  2. Intent Modeling:
    • Derive event/style signals from queries; apply filters for budget, category, color, and availability.
  3. LLM Prompt & Behavior Design:
    • Structure outputs as ranked tables with “Why it works” rationales; enforce naming and tone rules.
  4. Prototype Implementation:
    • Deploy an interactive Streamlit app to simulate customer journeys and stakeholder demos.
  5. Evaluation & Iteration:
    • Validate recommendation quality and response consistency; refine prompts, weights, and filters.

Expected Results & Impact

  • Improved Discovery: Faster path to relevant items through event- and influencer-aware curation.
  • Higher Engagement: Clear rationales and stylist-like tone encourage exploration and saves.
  • Conversion Lift Potential: Personalization logic designed to surface on-brand, in-stock options aligned to shopper profiles.
  • Roadmap Readiness: A working prototype that informs data requirements, guardrails, and production integration steps.

Content Acquisition Optimization for Streaming Profitability

Modeled film licensing decisions under budget and diversity constraints to maximize portfolio profit.

Summary

Built a decision support model in Excel that optimized film licensing decisions for a streaming platform. The system used binary integer optimization through Excel’s Solver to identify an ideal 34-film portfolio that maximized profit while satisfying real-world business constraints.

Problem Context

Streaming platforms face intense competition for subscribers while managing high licensing costs and limited budgets. The challenge was to select a minimum of 25 films to license under financial and diversity constraints. Traditional decision-making often overlooks trade-offs between profitability and variety, so we built a quantitative model to simulate this process using optimization principles.

Tools & Skills

  • Excel (Solver – Evolutionary Algorithm)
  • Binary Integer Programming (Knapsack Optimization)
  • Predictive Modeling (Cost & Revenue Estimation)
  • Data Cleaning & Scenario Analysis

Data & Constraints

Dataset: ~4,800 films from Kaggle → filtered to the Top 100 most popular titles.

  • Key Variables: title, genre, director, budget, popularity, revenue.
  • Constraints:
    • Licensing Budget: $405M
    • Minimum Revenue: $500M
    • Target Profit Margin: ≥ 19%
    • Minimum Film Count: 25 titles
    • Diversity: Penalty functions for duplicate genres/directors (to ensure film diversity)

Approach

  1. Data Cleaning & Preparation:
    • Cleaned and standardized the dataset, retaining the 100 most popular films.
    • Removed irrelevant or missing records and ensured consistency across key variables such as title, budget, and revenue.
  2. Popularity Normalization & Scoring:
    • Created a normalized popularity index (0–1 scale) to weigh film popularity and used it to generate a “popularity rate” adjustment factor for cost and revenue estimation.
  3. Predictive Modeling:
    • Applied formulas using each film’s production budget and popularity rate to estimate licensing cost.
    • Predicted potential streaming revenue as a function of normalized popularity and total revenue to create consistent financial proxies.
  4. Optimization Design:
    • Defined binary decision variables (1 = license, 0 = do not license).
    • Incorporated constraints for budget, minimum film count, and penalties for genre/director duplication to ensure diversity.
  5. Solver Execution:
    • Formulated an objective to maximize total profit = estimated revenue − estimated cost − penalties.
    • Used Excel’s Evolutionary Solver (Genetic Algorithm) to test thousands of portfolio combinations and select the optimal 34-film portfolio.

Results & Impact

  • Optimal Portfolio: 34 films across 32 genres and 27 directors
  • Projected Total Profit: ≈ $229M
  • Projected Profit Margin: ≈ 40%
  • Penalty Impact: ~$9.75M in diversity penalties offset by high-profit titles
  • Model Preference: favored high-return franchises even when diversity penalties applied, illustrating balance between commercial success and content diversity.
  • Decision Support: guided strategic content acquisition, reducing guesswork and improving ROI in high-stakes licensing decisions.

Delta Air Lines Star-Schema Data Warehouse & BI

Designed a data warehouse and business intelligence (DW/BI) system for the passenger airline division using a bus-matrix and conformed dimensions to power dashboards and decision-making.

Summary

Developed a multidimensional data warehouse and BI solution for Delta Air Lines’ passenger airline division. The design incorporated a high-level and detailed bus matrix, conformed dimensions, star schemas, transformation rules, and an MDDB cube, resulting in role-based Tableau dashboards for analysts, operational staff, and executives.

Problem Context

Delta operates complex, high-volume processes (flight operations, booking & ticketing, crew, baggage, loyalty) where siloed data hinders timely, consistent insights. The DW/BI initiative aimed to optimize flight operations, personalize customer experiences, streamline maintenance scheduling, improve on-time performance, track pilot performance, and manage costs across the network.

Tools & Skills

  • Tableau (role-based dashboards, KPI design)
  • Draw.io (ERD design)
  • Dimensional Modeling (bus matrix, conformed dimensions, star schema)
  • ETL & Transformation Rules
  • Aggregate Tables & MDDB Cube (performance layer for fast analytics)

Data & Constraints

Dataset: Flight operations and performance data obtained from the Delta Virtual Airlines Statistics platform.

  • Subject Areas: flight operations, customer booking & ticketing, baggage handling, crew management, delay management, loyalty, maintenance, support.
    • Each subject area was modeled as a distinct fact table with related dimensions capturing the key operational variables for that process.
  • Core Dimensions: Date & Time, Route, Aircraft, Pilot/Crew, Flight Status, Airport, Fare Class, Booking Channel, Weather.
  • Grain Example (Flight Operations Fact): One row per flight operation by route; measures include delay duration, flight length, distance traveled, and fuel consumption.
  • Design Constraints:
    • Conformed dimensions across subject areas for consistent KPIs.
    • Star schemas aligned to clearly defined grain and surrogate keys.
    • ETL stages with transformation rules for data standardization.
    • Aggregate tables and an MDDB cube to accelerate common analyses.

Approach

  1. Business Process Discovery & Scoping:
    • Mapped core processes and priorities through a High-Level Bus Matrix, Opportunity Matrix, and Prioritization Grid.
  2. Dimensional Modeling:
    • Authored Detailed Bus Matrix and star schemas with conformed dimensions to harmonize metrics across subject areas.
  3. Logical & Physical Design:
    • Defined fact table grain, keys, and measures; detailed dimension attributes and hierarchies.
  4. ETL & Transformation Rules:
    • Specified cleansing, conformance, and derivations to standardize and integrate disparate data sources.
  5. Performance Layer:
    • Designed aggregate table strategies and an MDDB cube for frequent flight-operations analyses.
  6. User & Task Analysis → Dashboards:
    • Built Tableau dashboards tailored for analysts (drill-downs), operational teams (situational awareness), and managers (KPI tracking and resource planning).

Results & Impact

  • Integrated Analytics: Conformed dimensions ensured consistent KPIs across divisions.
  • Operational Visibility: Exposed patterns in flight delays, high-traffic routes, and airport rankings for targeted improvements.
  • Decision Support: Enabled faster queries through aggregates and the MDDB cube, powering role-based dashboards that improve planning, operations, and executive insight.
  • Performance Efficiency: Optimized query performance and dashboard responsiveness through aggregate table strategies and multidimensional modeling.
  • Scalability Foundation: Designed a reusable DW/BI architecture that can easily expand to new subject areas such as maintenance or loyalty analytics.

New Medical Device Profitability Simulation

Performed a Monte Carlo–based “what-if” analysis to estimate first-year profit potential and assess risk for MedicTronix’s new device.

Summary

Conducted a first-year profit simulation for MedicTronix’s new medical device to quantify expected profitability and risk under uncertain cost and demand conditions. The analysis applied a Monte Carlo “what-if” approach with 1,000 simulated iterations to model revenue, costs, and potential profit outcomes.

Problem Context

MedicTronix required a method to forecast first-year profitability amid uncertainty in direct labor cost, parts cost, and demand. A probabilistic model in Excel was used to capture parameter variability, simulate a wide range of financial outcomes, and identify both best- and worst-case profit scenarios.

Tools & Skills

  • Excel (RAND, VLOOKUP, NORM.INV, AVERAGE, STDEV.S, COUNTIF)
  • Monte Carlo Analysis (1,000 iterations of what-if analysis)
  • Scenario Modeling (profit distributions & input ranges)
  • Statistical Analysis & Visualization (frequency table, profit distribution chart)

Data & Constraints

Dataset: Simulated operational and cost data for MedicTronix’s device based on market research and financial estimates.

  • Key Parameters (Expected Values):
    • Selling Price per Unit: $2,495
    • Administrative & Advertising Costs (fixed): $5,000,000
    • Direct Labor Cost per Unit (mean): $1,400
    • Parts Cost per Unit (mean): $350
    • First-Year Demand (mean): 25,000 units
  • Range Inputs (Simulation Ranges):
    • Direct Labor Cost per Unit: $1,200 – $1,600
    • Parts Cost per Unit (uniform distribution): $250 – $500
    • Demand (normally distributed): 0 – 50,000 units

Approach

  1. Model Design & Parameterization:
    • Defined a probabilistic profit model linking revenue, variable costs, and fixed costs into a profit equation.
    • Incorporated uncertainty via randomized sampling for labor, parts, and demand inputs.
  2. Simulation Execution:
    • Ran 1,000 Monte Carlo iterations to generate the profit distribution and summary statistics (mean, variability, and bounds).
  3. Statistical Evaluation:
    • Computed descriptive statistics (mean, standard deviation, minimum and maximum profit, probability of loss).
  4. Visualization & Reporting:
    • Plotted a profit frequency distribution to display probability density across the observed profit outcomes.

Results & Impact

  • Expected Profit: ≈ $13,029,542
  • Risk of Loss: No risk of loss (P = 0%)
  • Range of Outcomes: $3.7 M (min) – $25.9 M (max)
  • Most Frequent Outcome: ≈ $15 M profit per simulation cluster
  • Profit Potential: demonstrated strong profitability and resilience under cost-inflation scenarios.
  • Investment Decision: recommended proceeding with development and prototype investment due to high market and financial viability.
